Explanation

This year we threw the challenge of horsey prediction to our wider team who have used a range of modelling techniques to hunt down our top horses for 2015. Here we bring you details on what was done and the pros and cons of each approach.

The approaches we used this year include the tried-and-tested linear regression model, a new machine learning model, and another more unique approach. However before starting the modelling we gathered a lot of data and expert input to hone in on what matters when it comes to picking winners.

First up, we used web scraping scripts to gather as much data as we could for over 50 thousand horses and over 1 million race results (race with 24 horses would have 24 race results). Having such a big dataset allowed us to thoroughly test the model’s validity before including variables that we believed to be useful.

The next step was to understand the intersection of variables that matter (via. research) and variables we were able to collect information on. This intersection was made up of race odds, horse speed & form, weight carried, track condition preference of the horse, and barrier position in each race.

Armed with both the data and a hypothesis of what matters when it comes to predicting races the team set off from an even playing field to predict the top performing horses.
